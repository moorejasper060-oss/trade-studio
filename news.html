Remote access is built in. This release ships the desktop half of Trade Studio Mobile: turn it on in Settings and your computer can pair with the upcoming iPhone app over an end-to-end encrypted link — the relay only ever sees ciphertext, and pairing needs your license key plus a one-time code shown on your screen. And a heads-up on pricing: standard plans are moving to €14/mo (Solo) and €29/mo (Plus) — subscribe before August 4 and you keep today’s €9/€19 for as long as you stay subscribed.

New

Trade Studio Mobile — your bots in your pocket

We’re building a native iPhone app. It does two things. First, a phone-local paper-trading sim — the same SMA-ensemble crypto strategy your desktop bots run, replayed on your phone against real daily prices, with no account and no risk. Because iOS can’t trade in the background, it trades once a day and deterministically catches up the moment you open it, so you always see exactly where a live run would be.

Second, secure remote control of the bots running on your computer: see your live fleet and pause, resume or flatten any bot from anywhere (while your computer is on). Pairing needs your license key plus a one-time code shown on your desktop, and everything between your phone and your computer is end-to-end encrypted — the relay in the middle only ever sees ciphertext, so your keys never leave your devices. Remote control will be included with any subscription; the paper sim is free. Coming to the App Store.

July 2026 · v1.4.1
Safety

Closing Trade Studio now stops your bots

Quitting Trade Studio now shuts every bot down, and when you reopen the app your bots are listed stopped — nothing trades again until you press Start. Before this update, bots that were running when you quit would quietly start trading again the moment the app reopened.

There’s a safety net for crashes too: if the app ever dies unexpectedly, each bot notices within about half a minute and stops itself — a bot can never keep trading invisibly in the background while Trade Studio is closed.

One thing hasn’t changed: the window’s ✕ just hides Trade Studio to the tray, where your bots keep working. Use Quit (tray or Settings) when you want everything to stop.

June 2026 · v1.3.9
Fix

A running bot stays running

On Mac, a bot you’d started could quietly pause itself — usually after the computer slept and woke, or if the bot was automatically restarted in the background. The reason: a stock bot only remembered “running vs paused” in memory, so any restart reset it to paused. It now saves that state to disk, exactly like crypto bots already did, so a running bot comes back running after a sleep, a relaunch, or a crash-recovery restart. A bot you deliberately paused stays paused, and a brand-new bot still waits for you to press play the first time — we never start trading on our own. No change to how your bots trade.

Small crypto buys now show up on the dashboard

On the practice crypto simulator, a small test buy could go through and say it bought — but no position would appear in your live holdings, which made it look like the order had vanished. A top-to-bottom reliability and safety pass

We put the whole app through a deep audit and fixed everything it turned up — dozens of fixes in a single release, all aimed at one promise: when it comes to your money and your data, Trade Studio should never lose track and never fail quietly. None of this changes how your bots decide to trade — it makes the app underneath them much sturdier.

June 2026 · v1.2.19
New strategy

Swing Trend — a slower, daily strategy that rides the big moves

Most trading bots dart in and out all day. Our research keeps pointing the other way: the steadier edge is in slower, daily trend-following. Swing Trend is Trade Studio's first daily strategy — it buys a stock that breaks out to a new 55-day high while its longer-term trend is pointing up, then holds on and rides the move with a wide trailing stop, letting winners run for weeks.

It works very differently from the intraday strategies, so it's upfront about that. Swing Trend checks for a new trade only once a day, just after the US market opens — so keep Trade Studio running at that time so it doesn't miss its entry. It trades very rarely (often weeks between trades — that's by design, not the bot sitting idle) and it holds positions overnight, which means it carries overnight and gap risk. It's long-only, US stocks only, and we strongly recommend running it on paper first. You'll find it under Strategy on any stock bot, with a clear notice explaining exactly how it behaves. Classic and VWAP Reclaim are unchanged.

This release also adds a quiet safety improvement: Trade Studio now refuses to open a second copy of itself, so two windows can never end up fighting over the same accounts.

June 2026 · v1.2.13
New strategy

New strategy: VWAP Reclaim

Trade Studio now lets you choose how each bot trades. Alongside the original Classic Trend engine, there's a new intraday strategy called VWAP Reclaim. It waits for a stock to lose its session VWAP — the volume-weighted average price the market anchors to — then buys the reclaim when price pushes back above it inside an uptrend. Winners ride a wide, ATR-based target, and every position is flattened by the close, so you carry no overnight risk.

We selected it from research across roughly 130 strategies as the strongest intraday setup that still held up after realistic trading costs. It ships with its own curated, high-volatility watchlist — high-beta movers and liquid leveraged ETFs — because that's where the edge tends to live. Choose it per-bot in the strategy panel, with Patient, Balanced and Aggressive presets. It's long-only, so treat it as one to get to know on paper first — it's available now to paper trade.
